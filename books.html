✧･ﾟ: *✧･ﾟ:* Books I've read that made me want to share ✧･ﾟ: *✧･ﾟ:*

Maskiner der tænker

Algoritmernes hemmeligheder og vejen til kunstig intelligens

Inga Strümke, 2024

I loved this book! Inge Strümke is a Norwegian physicist thoroughly explaining AI in a way even non-techies can understand. I felt both very entertained and intrigued the whole book through, and will probably read it again soon. For anyone who is curious (or even a little afraid of) AI technology and how it works.

Wintering

The power of rest and retreat in difficult times

Katherine May, 2024

I read this book at a perfect time, when all I wanted to do was sit inside and craft and read and drink coffee under a warm blanket. Katherine May describes her own internal winter through beautiful nature metaphors and anecdotes and it's the perfect book to read if you want to slow down and turn inwards.

The Dispossessed

Ursula K. Le Guin, 1974

I loooooove Ursula Le Guin, her books speak to my soul! This one especially since it transports us to a world structured on anarchist principles and the social commentary is amazing. The way she paints the contrasting capitalist world is hilarious (and a little sad since we are totally living in it), and I could read it again and again.

Manhunt

Gretchen Felker-Martin, 2022

This books is both incredibly funny, scary, thought-provoking, heartbreaking and everything in between. I was laughing and crying in tandem. Set in a post-apocalyptic world where everyone with a certain level of testosterone in their body has been overcome by a wild virus, and written from the perspective of two incredible trans women, you get the thrill of body horror and the tender trans experience all mixed up in love stories and friendships and an absolutely hilarious scene where J.K. Rowling is violently murdered (sorry for the spoiler). Just read it.

The Year of Blue Water

Yanyi, 2019

Beautiful poems by trans writer and poet, Yanyi. I keep coming back to this collection, because it so effortlessly captures moving between identities and the uncertainty of that.

Translation State

Ann Leckie, 2023

OK, this is not my favourite scifi of all time, but something I think Ann Leckie did really well and that I enjoyed very much was the fluidity of gender in the characters of this story. Definitely worth a read!

The Books of Earthsea

Ursula K. Le Guin, 1968-2001

So, this book series is actually 5 different books written over a span of 40 years (!!). It's incredible. I read all five books in one summer, and I was so sad to be done. If you love Harry Potter but hate J.K. Rowling, these books are for you. If you love Lord of The Rings, but you want strong female characters, these books are for you.

Dawn

A Lilith's Brood novel

Octavia E. Butler, 1987

Ooof this book was surprisingly hot. Human-alien relationships, extremely descriptive alien worlds, non-binarism and polyamory, black female lead, all great things about this book. It is part of a three book series (I have not read the sequels), so if you like it you can just continue reading.

The Vegetarian

Han Kang, 2007

Interesting and quite disturbing story of a woman's decision to become a vegetarian. Set in Korea and told in three parts from three different perspectives, the story touches on themes of shame, control, desire and mental illness. I love the way Han Kang writes, it is honestly mesmerizing. Slight trigger warning though, SA.

On Earth We're Briefly Gorgeous

Ocean Vuong, 2019

Perhaps one of the most beautiful books I've ever read, written in letter-form as an intimate story of generational trauma, identity, queerness and immigration. Ocean Vuong writes so incredibly beautiful books and poems, I recommend everything he writes, truly.

The One Hundred Nights of Hero

Isabel Greenberg, 2016

Extremely funny and beautiful graphic novel full of stories told from women's perspective. The artwork is so interesting and the stories are funny, sweet, sad, magical and folktale-like.

Persepolis

Marjane Satrapi, 2000-2003

Another really cool graphic novel telling the story of the Iranian revolution from the perspective of young Marjane. Very cool black and white artwork and beautiful story of resilience and solidarity.

The Left Hand of Darkness

Ursula K. Le Guin, 1969

Also a great Ursula Le Guin book, still set in the same universe as her other scifi novels. Very interesting take on gender, although some criticism is appropriate. If you like books with a bit of gender-fuckery I recommend this one.

We the Animals

Justin Torres, 2011

Beautiful queer coming-of-age story that explores themes of violence, family bonds, childhood trauma, siblinghood and sexuality. Not a light read, but incredibly moving.
